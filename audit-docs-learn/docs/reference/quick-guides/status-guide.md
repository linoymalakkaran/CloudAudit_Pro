## Recommended core statuses

### Not started

Meaning:

- No work has begun, or work was intentionally deferred.

### In progress

Meaning:

- Work is actively being performed.

Good practice:

- Keep partial notes as you go (what you tested, whatâ€™s left).
- Attach evidence incrementally instead of at the end.

### Needs review (or Ready for review)

Meaning:

- The preparer believes the work is complete enough for reviewer sign-off.

Before moving to review, ensure:

- Steps have conclusions (not just evidence)
- Exceptions are explained and cross-referenced
- Evidence is attached and readable

Process context: [Review & Quality Control](/docs/processes/review-quality-control)

### Returned with notes (or Rework required)

Meaning:

- Reviewer found issues that require updates before approval.

Good practice:

- Capture reviewer notes in one place (avoid scattered comments)
- When resubmitting, summarize what changed

### Complete (or Approved)

Meaning:

- Work is finished and accepted at the current review level.

Good practice:

- Avoid reopening completed items unless there is a real scope change.
- If reopened, record why (audit trail).
