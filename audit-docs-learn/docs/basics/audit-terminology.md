## üéØ Core Audit Concepts

### Audit
**Simple Definition**: A systematic examination of financial records to verify accuracy  
**In Plain English**: Checking if a company's financial statements are truthful and accurate  
**Example**: "We need to conduct an annual audit of XYZ Corporation's finances"

### Auditor
**Simple Definition**: A person who performs audits  
**In Plain English**: The financial detective who checks if numbers are correct  
**Types**:
- **External Auditor**: Works for audit firm, independent
- **Internal Auditor**: Works for the company being audited

### Audit Opinion
**Simple Definition**: The auditor's conclusion about financial statements  
**In Plain English**: The final grade the auditor gives  
**Four Types**:
1. **Unqualified (Clean)** ‚úÖ - Everything looks good!
2. **Qualified** ‚ö†Ô∏è - Mostly good with some exceptions
3. **Adverse** ‚ùå - Significant problems found
4. **Disclaimer** ‚ùì - Couldn't get enough information

### Materiality
**Simple Definition**: The significance level of an error or omission  
**In Plain English**: How big does an error need to be before it matters?  
**Example**: 
```
Company Revenue: $10,000,000
Material amount (typically 5%): $500,000

$1,000 error ‚Üí Not material (ignore it)
$600,000 error ‚Üí Material (must investigate!)
```

### Audit Risk
**Simple Definition**: The chance that auditor issues wrong opinion  
**In Plain English**: The risk of missing something important  
**Components**:
- **Inherent Risk**: Risk built into the business
- **Control Risk**: Risk controls won't work  
- **Detection Risk**: Risk auditor won't find error

## üíº Financial Statement Terms

### Balance Sheet
**Simple Definition**: Snapshot of what company owns and owes at a point in time  
**In Plain English**: A financial photograph showing assets, liabilities, and equity  
**Formula**: `Assets = Liabilities + Equity`

**Example**:
| Assets | Amount | Liabilities & Equity | Amount |
|--------|---------|----------------------|---------|
| Cash | $50,000 | Loans Payable | $30,000 |
| Inventory | $30,000 | Equity | $50,000 |
| **Total** | **$80,000** | **Total** | **$80,000** |

### Income Statement (Profit & Loss)
**Simple Definition**: Shows revenue and expenses over a period  
**In Plain English**: Did the company make or lose money this year?  
**Formula**: `Revenue - Expenses = Net Income (Profit/Loss)`

**Example**:
```
Revenue (Sales):        $500,000
Expenses:              -$350,000

Net Income (Profit):    $150,000
```

### Trial Balance
**Simple Definition**: List of all account balances at a specific date  
**In Plain English**: A complete list of every account with its balance  
**Purpose**: Starting point for creating financial statements

**Example**:
| Account | Debit | Credit |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Cash | $50,000 | |
| Accounts Receivable | $30,000 | |
| Revenue | | $80,000 |
| **Totals** | **$80,000** | **$80,000** |

:::info Why "Trial"?
It's called "trial" because debits and credits must balance (trial = test).  
If they don't balance, there's an error that must be fixed!
:::

### Assets
**Simple Definition**: Things the company owns that have value  
**In Plain English**: Stuff that could be sold or used to generate money  
**Types**:
- **Current Assets**: Cash or convertible to cash within a year (cash, inventory, receivables)
- **Fixed Assets**: Long-term items (buildings, equipment, vehicles)
- **Intangible Assets**: Non-physical valuable items (patents, trademarks, goodwill)

### Liabilities
**Simple Definition**: What the company owes to others  
**In Plain English**: Debts and obligations that must be paid  
**Types**:
- **Current Liabilities**: Due within a year (payables, short-term loans)
- **Long-term Liabilities**: Due after a year (mortgages, bonds)

### Equity
**Simple Definition**: The owners' stake in the company  
**In Plain English**: What's left after subtracting debts from assets  
**Formula**: `Equity = Assets - Liabilities`  
**Also called**: Net worth, shareholders' equity, owner's equity

## üìù Accounting Fundamentals

### Debit and Credit
**Simple Definition**: The two sides of accounting entries  
**In Plain English**: Left side (debit) and right side (credit) of accounting equation

**The Rules**:
| Account Type | Increase | Decrease |
|--------------|----------|----------|
| Assets | Debit | Credit |
| Liabilities | Credit | Debit |
| Equity | Credit | Debit |
| Revenue | Credit | Debit |
| Expenses | Debit | Credit |

:::tip Easy Way to Remember
- **DEA**: Debits increase Expenses and Assets
- **LIC**: Credits increase Liabilities, Income (Revenue), and Capital (Equity)
:::

### Journal Entry
**Simple Definition**: Record of a business transaction  
**In Plain English**: Writing down what happened to accounts when something occurs

**Example**: Company buys $1,000 of inventory with cash
```
Date: Jan 15, 2024
Debit: Inventory         $1,000
Credit: Cash             $1,000
Description: Purchased inventory for cash
```

### Chart of Accounts
**Simple Definition**: Organized list of all accounts company uses  
**In Plain English**: The master list of all account names and numbers

**Example**:
```
1000 - Assets
  1100 - Cash
  1200 - Accounts Receivable
  1300 - Inventory
2000 - Liabilities
  2100 - Accounts Payable
  2200 - Loans Payable
3000 - Equity
  3100 - Owner's Capital
```

### Posting
**Simple Definition**: Transferring journal entries to ledger accounts  
**In Plain English**: Moving transactions from the journal (diary) to account pages

## üîç Audit Procedures & Evidence

### Audit Procedure
**Simple Definition**: Specific test or check auditor performs  
**In Plain English**: A task the auditor does to verify something  
**Examples**:
- Review bank reconciliation
- Count physical inventory
- Confirm account balances with customers

### Audit Evidence
**Simple Definition**: Information auditor uses to form opinion  
**In Plain English**: Proof that numbers are correct  
**Types**:
- Documents (invoices, contracts)
- Confirmations (letters from banks)
- Physical observation (counting inventory)
- Calculations (rechecking math)

### Sampling
**Simple Definition**: Examining a subset of transactions instead of all  
**In Plain English**: Checking some items to judge the whole group  
**Example**: Check 50 out of 1,000 invoices to assess accuracy

### Substantive Testing
**Simple Definition**: Direct tests of account balances and transactions  
**In Plain English**: Actually checking the numbers themselves  
**Example**: Verifying invoices add up to revenue total

### Test of Controls
**Simple Definition**: Checking if company's processes work properly  
**In Plain English**: Testing if safety measures are functioning  
**Example**: Verifying purchase orders are properly approved

## üìä Audit Process Terms

### Engagement
**Simple Definition**: The audit assignment/contract  
**In Plain English**: The specific audit job the auditor is hired to do  
**Example**: "We accepted the engagement to audit ABC Corp for 2024"

### Audit Planning
**Simple Definition**: Preparing strategy for how audit will be conducted  
**In Plain English**: Creating the audit game plan  
**Includes**:
- Understanding the business
- Identifying risks
- Determining procedures
- Assigning team members

### Fieldwork
**Simple Definition**: The period when auditors are actively gathering evidence  
**In Plain English**: When auditors are "in the field" doing testing  
**Typically**: Several weeks at client's location

### Workpapers (Working Papers)
**Simple Definition**: Documentation of audit work performed  
**In Plain English**: The auditor's notes, calculations, and evidence  
**Purpose**: Prove audit was done properly and support conclusions

### Finding
**Simple Definition**: An issue or discrepancy discovered during audit  
**In Plain English**: Something wrong or unusual that was found  
**Severity Levels**:
- **Critical** üî¥ - Major problem requiring immediate attention
- **High** üü† - Significant issue
- **Medium** üü° - Moderate concern
- **Low** üü¢ - Minor issue

### Management Letter
**Simple Definition**: Auditor's recommendations to company management  
**In Plain English**: Suggestions for improving processes and controls  
**Not**: The official audit opinion

## üìÖ Audit Timeline Terms

### Fiscal Year
**Simple Definition**: Company's 12-month accounting period  
**In Plain English**: The company's "year" for accounting (may not be Jan-Dec)  
**Example**: July 1, 2024 - June 30, 2025

### Year-End Audit
**Simple Definition**: Audit of full year financial statements  
**When**: After fiscal year closes  
**Scope**: Complete

### Interim Audit
**Simple Definition**: Audit of partial period (often quarterly or semi-annual)  
**When**: During the year  
**Scope**: Limited

### Prior Year / Comparative
**Simple Definition**: Previous period's numbers  
**Purpose**: Compare this year to last year to spot unusual changes  
**Example**: 2024 results compared to 2023
